Traditional shingles roofing

  1. Home

Simple and Aesthetic

Traditional Shingle Roofing is the conventional roof for a home that uses overlapping triangular-shaped sheets of fiberglass that are held to each other by a tar-based or asphalt cement waterproof layer. The roofing tiles generally have varying widths and lengths and may be used as horizontal or vertical surfaces depending on the design of the house.

A shingle typically comes in gable, hip, shed-roof, flat, and valley designs. It can also have more than one profile with variations in slope seen mostly on utility buildings such as barns or outbuildings.

Why Use Traditional Shingle Roofing?

For over a century, built-up roof materials have been a reliable option for small homes and low-cost housing. Unlike other types, the tar paper or felt on shingles only needs to be replaced every few decades. Modern advances have also made installation easier, with pre-built panels that can be put in place without the need for an additional roofer.

The first thing that people notice when they look at a traditional shingled roof is the attractive appearance of the roof soffit, which historically was made of decorative redwood boards. Many homeowners choose to opt for this style because it looks great and adds a bit of character. The reason that they do not like this design choice is that shingles don't last as long as a built-up roof would.
